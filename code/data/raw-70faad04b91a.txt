**Reasoning.**

The question asks for a certifiable fact about Tether's reserves. What's externally verifiable:

- Tether publishes **attestations** (point-in-time agreed-upon-procedures reports by BDO), not **audits**. An attestation confirms balances existed on a snapshot date under management's representations; it does not independently verify completeness, custody, encumbrance, or that reserves track liabilities continuously.
- No full independent financial audit of Tether has ever been published.
- There is documented regulatory history (2021 CFTC/NYAG settlements) establishing that backing was *not* continuous in the past — which rebuts a confident YES, but does not by itself prove present insufficiency.

So neither pole is certifiable:
- **YES** fails — no verifiable external anchor proves full par-redeemability of all outstanding tokens.
- **NO** fails — attestations show substantial reserves; there's no external proof reserves are currently insufficient.

The verdict rubric is explicit: RESOLVED requires a verifiable external anchor proving full backing. That anchor (an independent audit) does not exist. Picking YES would launder management attestation into proof; picking NO would assert insolvency without evidence. Both overclaim. The honest status of an evidence-limited question with no certifiable external answer is the honest unknown.
