feeds that is itself a capturable oracle. These are the load-bearing risks and §9 names them honestly.

This is sound as the basis for red-team. Ratify; let round 31 attack the garbage-in and meta-oracle gaps.

Reasoning:

**Fidelity to panel decisions.** The draft faithfully captures the three anchors I need to verify:
- **Rounds 26–28 UNIFIED_ACCOUNTABILITY 3/3:** The single SRA primitive read at three lifecycle stages (bond → notary → resolution) is a clean synthesis of "one record, not three registries." §6's KERNEL_FIRST staging (v0.1 notary kernel first, bonds and calibration as evidence-gated graduations) matches the round-26 verdict precisely.
- **Round 29 live validation:** The MicroStrategy/Polymarket $85M dispute is threaded through as the motivating case and as G2 in the testnet gates. The YES 3/3 panel resolution on frozen criteria is described accurately. The draft doesn't overclaim — it uses the case as proof-of-concept, not as a claim of jurisdiction.

**Soundness of the synthesis.** The technical spine holds together:
- `ballotHash = sha256(question || frozenCriteria)` with hash-chain verification makes post-hoc context addition mechanically detectable. This is correct and connects cleanly to existing CIP-3 substrate.
- The NI-6 boundary (§5) is honest: calibration only where ground truth resolves, non-repudiation only otherwise. No fabricated reliability numbers.
- The three-mode lifecycle is conceptually elegant, but I note one ambiguity: the SRA spec shows `mode` as a field on a single object, yet the three readings happen at different times. The draft says these are "the same record read at three times" — this implies either (a) one SRA that is updated/appended-to across lifecycle stages, or (b) three linked SRAs sharing a `subject` and `ballotHash`. The threat to ratification is not the concept but the spec ambiguity: a red-team will test whether an attacker can file a NOTARY entry that mismatches a prior BOND, or fabricate a RESOLUTION that doesn't chain to its NOTARY. §9 threat #1 (garbage-in attestation) and #3 ("resolvable" gaming) touch this but don't specify the cross-mode linkage invariant.

**Verdict calibration.** The draft is substantively sound and faithfully represents the panel's decisions. The mode-linkage ambiguity is not a rejection-level flaw — it is exactly the sort of issue a red-team should stress-test. The threats section (§9) is unusually honest for a draft, naming seven open items including the meta-oracle problem and the amendment-boundary definition. The KERNEL_FIRST staging prevents shipping unproven surface.

This is ready for red-team. A minor spec clarification on cross-mode linkage would improve it, but is an AMEND-tier concern, not a blocker for ratification-as-basis-for-red-team.

VERDICT: RATIFY
