## The gap (precise, real, fixable)
**The "gate" does not gate — it classifies and always exits 0.** The headers claim *"this gates it; nothing is approved without 2/3 SOUND… Every other outcome **blocks** it… mechanical, not a human reading the tally."* But mechanically:

1. The agent has *already committed* to HEAD before `run-self-improve.ts` runs. On `REJECTED`/`CHANGES_REQUESTED`/`INCONCLUSIVE` nothing is reverted, blocked, or undone — the change stays in HEAD.
2. The *only* output is `console.log("GATE: …")`. `main()` returns normally for every outcome, so the **process exits 0 whether the verdict is APPROVED or REJECTED.** No `process.exitCode`, no throw, no non-zero signal.

So for a cycle whose stated purpose is "run after a commit, or on a cadence" with "no human in the loop," the gate result is *only* consumable by a human reading the tally — the exact thing the commit says it replaces. Any wrapper/scheduler/CI step cannot distinguish a ratified-SOUND from a REJECTED. The decision is correct; it just isn't *actionable*, which is the difference between "blocks" (claimed) and "prints a label" (actual).

**Fix:** set a non-zero `process.exitCode` on any non-`APPROVED` gate (including the missing-`done` INCONCLUSIVE early return), so the standing cycle's caller can actually act on the verdict. That closes the claim-vs-behavior gap without touching the (correct) `gateOf` logic.
