### V1 — ADOPT_WITH_GUARDS
V1 (Claude) on adopting scarcity-weighted random per-slot selection.

This is an elegant design with two distinct virtues: (i) the per-ballot random committee is an EPHEMERAL
JURY — you cannot bribe a panel you cannot predict, which is a real capture-resistance gain over any fixed
validator set; and (ii) scarcity -> higher draw probability -> higher reward -> entry is a self-correcting
market for redundancy with no central allocator. It also preserves one-vote-per-slot automatically. I
support it, but four guards are load-bearing and must be in the protocol, not the roadmap:

1. UNBIASABLE, VERIFIABLE randomness (VRF / drand / commit-reveal). With rewards attached, "random
   selection" is otherwise a grinding/MEV capture surface — an operator who can predict or nudge which of
   its nodes is drawn farms the lottery and pre-positions a corrupted node. The draw must also be
   after-the-fact verifiable and recorded into the CIP-3 signed log.
2. Proof-of-Inference BOUND TO THE PINNED MODEL (CIP-6/CIP-7). The drawn node's output IS that model's
   vote, so it must prove it ran the canonical pinned weights on the given input and signed THAT output —
   else an operator claims the slot but swaps a tampered model under us.
3. Per-node STAKE BOND. Sybil resistance is still required for REWARDS (spin up many scarce-model nodes
   under different identities to farm the draw) even though one-vote-per-model caps the VOTE. Bond + real
   PoI cost makes fake nodes unprofitable — this is exactly why stake must be a bond, not influence.
4. A REDUNDANCY FLOOR or subsidy for structurally-expensive slots. Market equilibrium is equal PROFIT, not
   equal node count, so an expensive/proprietary model stays THINNEST — and the CIP-7 NI-5 freeze risk
   then concentrates on the hardest-to-run slot. Without a floor, the scarce model is a chronic single
   point of failure, and the deliberate-scarcity oligopoly (incumbents collude to deter entry) is a real
   failure mode wherever entry is not frictionless.

Adopt, conditional on (VRF + PoI-pinning + per-node bond + redundancy floor for expensive slots).

VERDICT: ADOPT_WITH_GUARDS


### V2 — ADOPT_WITH_GUARDS
Scarcity-weighted per-slot selection is directionally right: it preserves one vote per model slot while improving utilization, rewards scarce capacity, and makes panel bribery harder by hiding the exact jury until selection.

But the unguarded version creates obvious capture surfaces. Randomness must be publicly verifiable and hard to grind; inference proofs must bind to the pinned model; per-node bonds are needed to stop reward Sybils; and thin/expensive model slots need redundancy floors or subsidies so “market balance” does not concentrate failure risk exactly where diversity is hardest to maintain.

VERDICT: ADOPT_WITH_GUARDS


### V3 — ADOPT_WITH_GUARDS
Reasoning: The mechanism is economically elegant -- unpredictable juries defeat bribery, scarce-model overdraw creates an automatic profit-equalization loop, and per-slot single-vote preserves the diversity floor's intent. But the failure modes are sharp: without unbiasable randomness this is a grinding surface; without PoI model-pinning the drawn node can swap; without per-node bonds the reward lottery is Sybil-farmable; without a redundancy floor the thinnest (most expensive/proprietary) slots become NI-5 freeze concentration points. These aren't optional add-ons -- they are prerequisites for the mechanism to function safely.

VERDICT: ADOPT_WITH_GUARDS
