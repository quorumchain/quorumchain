"Self-reported but independently replayable" — RIGHT.** The distinction holds: cryptographic replay (hash-chained log, Ed25519 sigs, appendix commands) proves the computation is *reproducible from signed inputs* and untampered — it does **not** prove the panel's judgments are correct or that the inputs were externally generated. The word "independently-verifiable" is safe *because* it's qualified as verifiability of the log, not validation of the verdicts. Passes failure mode (i). One nit for prose: keep "independently-verifiable" tethered to the log every time it appears, so it never drifts into reading as third-party validation.

**2. **3. Model-count scaling — argument sound, one phrase overstated.** The diversity logic is correctly cited from §2: a 2/3 supermajority across ceil(2N/3) *distinct* families means more independent families monotonically raises capture cost and lowers correlated-error exposure. That justifies the **direction** (more) and the **ordering** (grow diversity before relying on it — also correctly placed in the roadmap). But the claim "they are not round numbers for their own sake" slightly overreaches: the diversity argument justifies *more*, not *exactly ~10 then ~20+*. The binding constraint isn't named — security scales with **genuine distinctness**, not headcount, and the real ceiling is the *supply* of genuinely-independent frontier families. Reaching "~20+" by adding correlated/fine-tuned variants of the same base is diversity-theater, the exact correlated-error risk §2 warns about. Fix in prose: present 10/20+ as engineering checkpoints bounded by available distinct families, and state explicitly that count without distinctness buys no security.

**Roadmap ordering — defensible, one clarity requirement.** Autonomy-done → economy → testnet → decentralization → mainnet is dependency-correct, and decentralization-before-mainnet with "grow the set before relying on it" is right (thin set cheaper to capture). The one place a reader could trip: phase 2 economic layer promises "real flows" and "deterministic execution" *before* the phase-3 testnet substrate exists. That only coheres if the execution venue is the external (already-live) launch chain, not Quorumchain's own chain — but the launch vehicle is out of scope. As written this risks reading as Quorumchain having on-chain execution at phase 2, contradicting phase 3 (scope-confusion, failure mode ii). Not a mis-order — a venue-disambiguation the prose must make explicit: phase-2 deterministic execution runs on an external chain; Quorumchain's own on-chain execution first appears at the testnet phase.

**Call:** No status claim is fatally overstated, scope is not mis-stated (it is, in fact, unusually carefully stated), and no phase is mis-ordered. The two items above — name the distinctness-supply constraint on model scaling, and disambiguate the phase-2 execution venue — are prose-tightening notes, not defects in the argument as specified. The framing is sound to draft.
