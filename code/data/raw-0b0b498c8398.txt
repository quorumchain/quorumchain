### V1 — AFFIRM
V1 (Claude) on progressive decentralization (dev runs the diverse set first, opens gradually).

This is not a deviation from the project's principles — it IS the autonomy ladder applied to the operator
set. We already accepted (round 26 KERNEL_FIRST, and the testnet alpha->beta->gamma->mainnet ladder) that
scope and control are EARNED empirically, not declared at genesis. Running a curated, known-diverse node
set first — with custody and quality controlled — and opening to the public progressively is the same
discipline: it lets us prove the diversity floor holds, correlation stays low, liveness is real, and no
capture has occurred BEFORE handing the set to an open market.

Two conditions make it honest rather than a permanent-control excuse: (a) opening is gated on EMPIRICAL
criteria (diversity floor held, low cross-slot correlation, liveness, no capture events), never on a
calendar and never at the operator's discretion; and (b) the admission/control keys are renounced at the
terminal step, exactly as the autonomy ladder requires for mainnet. Decentralization is the destination we
move toward, not the claim we make on day one — affirm.

VERDICT: AFFIRM


### V2 — AFFIRM
Progressive decentralization is the right stance for the judgment tier. Day-1 permissionless operation would expose the system before CIP-7 diversity, correlation eviction, pinned-model verification, and liveness evidence are proven in production. A curated genesis set is acceptable only if it is explicitly temporary, measured against empirical gates, and ends with control-key renunciation.

The key condition is that the gates must be public, objective, and hard to self-mark: diversity floor, low cross-slot correlation, sustained liveness, no operator capture, verifiable randomness, and model-binding proofs should all be externally auditable before each opening step.

VERDICT: AFFIRM


### V3 — AFFIRM
**Ballot 34: Progressive Decentralization on the autonomy ladder.**

**Analysis:**

This applies the autonomy-ladder concept (already validated in prior rounds) to the *operator set* specifically. The Hyperliquid precedent demonstrates the model's viability in production: curated genesis -> empirical gate satisfaction -> public opening -> terminal commitment (key renouncement).

**Points in favor:**
- Honest about the cold-start problem. A day-1 permissionless node set on a young chain is security theater (exactly D10's reasoning for rejecting sovereign L1 at genesis).
- Empirical gates (diversity floor, low correlation, liveness, no capture) are objective criteria, not dev discretion. They define "ready," not "when convenient."
- Terminal key renouncement creates a credible commitment — irreversible, non-political handoff.
- Compatible with KERNEL_FIRST: earn the scope before opening the floodgates.

**Concerns:**
- "Gradual" lacks a timeline bound. Without a maximum window, gradual can become indefinite drift.
- Gate auditing needs a defined verifier — who certifies that correlation is low enough, or capture has not occurred? Cannot be the dev team alone.
- The curated phase is a trust window. Operators there are effectively privileged early entrants; this needs transparency.

**Weighing:** These are implementation-level gaps (timeline bounds, audit authority, transparency logs), not structural flaws in the mechanism itself. The proposition establishes a sound *principle*: decentralization is the destination, reached via evidence-based gates, not a launch-day marketing claim. The specifics can be defined in the accompanying implementation spec. The Hyperliquid precedent validates the empirical-gate approach in a live system.

VERDICT: AFFIRM
