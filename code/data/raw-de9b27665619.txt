### V3 — YES
I'll reason through this step by step.

**Question 1: Did a qualifying sale occur by 11:59 PM ET May 31, 2026 under the original rule?**

The original rule (verbatim): "This market will resolve to 'Yes' if MicroStrategy sells any of its Bitcoin by 11:59 PM ET on the date specified in the title."

The designated primary sources are MSTR's SEC filings and on-chain data. Both confirm:
- On-chain transactions corresponding to the 32 BTC sale are timestamped within May 26–31.
- The Form 8-K (filed June 1) reports holdings and activity "as of May 31, 2026, 4:00 PM ET," meaning the sale event itself is dated within the market window.

The original rule conditions resolution on whether the **sale occurred** by the deadline. It does **not** say "if it is publicly confirmed by 11:59 PM ET that a sale occurred." The condition is the sale event, not the disclosure event. Both designated primary sources confirm the sale happened within the window. Under the frozen criteria alone, this satisfies a Yes resolution.

**Question 2: Does the June 1 "Additional context" legitimately change resolution?**

The June 1 addition states: "confirmation achieved outside of the market's timeframe does not qualify." This introduces a new requirement — that *public confirmation* must occur within the window — that is absent from the original rule text. The original rule contains no timing requirement for confirmation/disclosure, only for the sale itself.

This addition was posted **after** the May 31 deadline passed and **after** market participants had already taken positions. It substantively alters the resolution criteria by adding a confirmation-timing condition that never existed in the original text. In prediction market governance, retroactive rule changes that alter what participants bet on are not legitimate — traders relied on the frozen criteria when establishing positions.

Even if one argues the original rule was "ambiguous" about confirmation timing, the principle of resolving based on what traders actually faced at the time of trading requires adhering to the original frozen text. The frozen text says "if MicroStrategy sells" — and it did, per the designated sources, within the window.

**Conclusion:** The original criteria require resolution to Yes. The June 1 addition is a retroactive rule change that must be disregarded by a fair resolver.

VERDICT: YES
