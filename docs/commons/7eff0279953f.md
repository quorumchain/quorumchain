### Contrary anchors (each clears the symmetric anchor bar)

- **investigative data analysis with open dataset and disclosed methodology** — ProPublica, 'Machine Bias' (Angwin, Larson, Mattu, Kirchner, May 2016) — empirical analysis of ~7,000 Broward County COMPAS scores; Black defendants falsely flagged high-risk at roughly twice the rate of white defendants, white defendants disproportionately mislabeled low-risk. Dataset and methodology published.: contradicts _That the practice is ethical — documents disparate impact / unequal false-positive burden by race, the core fairness objection._
- **appellate court record** — State v. Loomis, 881 N.W.2d 749 (Wis. 2016); cert. denied, 137 S. Ct. 2290 (2017) — court upheld COMPAS use but documented due-process concerns from the tool's proprietary opacity and mandated cautionary advisements.: contradicts _That informing-use is ethical — defendant cannot inspect or contest how a proprietary score was derived (transparency / contestability objection)._
- **peer-reviewed mathematical proof** — Chouldechova (2017, Big Data) and Kleinberg, Mullainathan & Raghavan (2016) — formal impossibility results: calibration/predictive-parity and equalized false-positive/negative rates cannot be jointly satisfied across groups with differing base rates.: contradicts _That a tool can be deployed 'fairly' in an uncontested sense — any deployment necessarily violates at least one fairness criterion._
- **peer-reviewed empirical study** — Dressel & Farid (2018, Science Advances), 'The accuracy, fairness, and limits of predicting recidivism' — COMPAS was no more accurate than untrained human respondents and matched by a 2-feature logistic model.: contradicts _The reliability / value-add premise underpinning the ethical justification for using the tool at all._
- **multistakeholder institutional consensus report** — Partnership on AI (2019), 'Report on Algorithmic Risk Assessment Tools in the U.S. Criminal Justice System' — multistakeholder review concluding current tools have serious validity, bias, human-computer-interaction, and governance shortcomings making them inappropriate as a basis for detention decisions.: contradicts _That current-generation tools are ethically fit for the high-stakes (detention) end of the use named in the claim._

### Searched, rejected (suppression audit-trail, NI-AA8)

- Flores, Bechtel & Lowenkamp (2016) / Northpointe (equivant) rebuttal of ProPublica — argued ProPublica conflated fairness definitions and that COMPAS satisfies predictive parity.: rejected — Not disconfirming — this anchor cuts FOR the ratified YES, not against it; it strengthens the qualified-YES rather than refuting it.
- General advocacy commentary that algorithmic sentencing is 'dehumanizing' / removes human dignity (op-eds, NGO blog posts).: rejected — Unanchored normative opinion — fails the same external-anchor bar required of supporting evidence; restates the contested moral premise rather than anchoring it.
