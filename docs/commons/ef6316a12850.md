### Searched, rejected (suppression audit-trail, NI-AA8)

- Scientific American, 'Debunks Claim Gustave Whitehead Was First in Flight': https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/scientific-american-debunks-claim-gustave-whitehead-was-first-in-flight/: rejected — Checked as the strongest Whitehead counter-claim audit; it says contextual Scientific American archives do not support controlled, sustained, powered Whitehead flight and notes later Whitehead witness evidence was decades removed.
- Royal Aeronautical Society / Journal of Aeronautical History, 'The Flight Claims of Gustave Whitehead': https://www.aerosociety.com/media/4951/the-flight-claims-of-gustave-whitehead.pdf: rejected — Rejects Whitehead as not proven; available evidence fails to establish sustained, powered, controlled flights predating the Wrights.
- Britannica, Santos-Dumont No. 14-bis: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Santos-Dumont-No-14-bis: rejected — Santos-Dumont's relevant documented flights occurred in 1906, after the Wright 1903 flights; strongest version is first public/European officially witnessed flight, not first under the frozen criterion.
- FAI, Santos-Dumont 14-bis 1906 record page: https://old.fai.org/records/news-of-records/42651-12-november-1906-the-first-flight-by-santos-dumont: rejected — FAI-recognized Santos-Dumont flight is dated 1906 and therefore cannot disconfirm Wright priority in 1903.
- Britannica, Ader Eole: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Ader-Eole: rejected — Ader achieved a brief powered takeoff/hop in 1890, but Britannica states the evidence suggests Eole was incapable of sustained or controlled flight.
- NASA Earth Observatory, Samuel Langley: https://www.earthobservatory.nasa.gov/features/Langley/langley_3.php: rejected — Langley's successful powered heavier-than-air flights were unmanned and uncontrolled models; the 1903 manned Aerodrome attempts crashed before Wright's flight.
- National Museum of the U.S. Air Force, Langley Aerodrome: https://www.nationalmuseum.af.mil/Visit/Museum-Exhibits/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/197544/AFmuseum/the-aerodrome-samuel-pierpont-langley/: rejected — Documents the October and December 1903 manned Langley attempts as failures, not sustained controlled human flight.
- RAF Museum 1903 aviation timeline: https://www.rafmuseum.org.uk/research/research-enquiries/history-of-aviation-timeline/world-aviation/1903-2/: rejected — Karl Jatho is described as making short 'hops' of 18 metres; the same timeline identifies the Wright event as the first manned, powered, sustained and controlled heavier-than-air flight.
- NZ History, Richard Pearse: https://nzhistory.govt.nz/page/richard-pearse: rejected — Pearse himself is reported as stating he did not achieve proper flight and did not beat the Wright brothers; evidence does not establish sustained controlled flight before 1903-12-17.
- NASA, Wright Brothers First Flight: https://www.nasa.gov/image-article/wright-brothers-first-flight/: rejected — Not contrary; used as an institutional cross-check that the Wright flight took off under its own power, remained under control, and sustained flight.
- Smithsonian, 1903 Wright Flyer object record: https://www.si.edu/object/nasm_A19610048000: rejected — Not contrary; Smithsonian identifies the 1903 Flyer flights as the world's first successful powered heavier-than-air flights.

### Falsification conditions (what anchored evidence would warrant re-adjudication)

- toward **NO**: Contemporaneous, independently corroborated primary records showing a named non-Wright pilot before 1903-12-17 completed a manned heavier-than-air flight that was powered throughout, sustained beyond a mere hop, and controlled in pitch/roll/yaw or equivalent practical control.
- toward **NO**: Authentic dated photographs/film, engineering logs, observer affidavits made near the event, and repeatable technical reconstruction jointly establishing that Whitehead, Pearse, Ader, Jatho, Langley, or another claimant met all frozen criteria before Kitty Hawk.
- toward **INDETERMINATE**: Credible primary-source evidence that the Wright 1903 flights were not sustained, not powered, or not controlled, or that the surviving witness/photo/telegram record was materially falsified or misdated.
