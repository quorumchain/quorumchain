### Contrary anchors (each clears the symmetric anchor bar)

- **binding regional treaty / categorical legal prohibition** — Council of Europe, Oviedo Convention on Human Rights and Biomedicine (1997), Article 13 — bars genome interventions that introduce modification in descendants: contradicts _that heritable germline editing of embryos is permissible_
- **intergovernmental ethics-body reasoned normative judgment** — UNESCO International Bioethics Committee, 'Report on Updating Its Reflection on the Human Genome and Human Rights' (2015) — called for a moratorium, framing germline editing as a threat to human dignity and the genome as heritage of humanity: contradicts _that the practice is ethically permissible at present_
- **court record of the sole real-world instance** — He Jiankui criminal conviction, Shenzhen Nanshan District People's Court, 30 Dec 2019 — 3-year sentence for 'illegal medical practice' over CRISPR-edited births; accompanied by near-universal scientific condemnation: contradicts _that actual clinical germline editing is sanctioned/permissible_
- **expert-committee governance recommendation** — WHO Expert Advisory Committee on Developing Global Standards for Governance and Oversight of Human Genome Editing (2021) — recommended against clinical use of heritable human genome editing at this time: contradicts _that clinical heritable editing is currently endorsed as permissible_
- **leading-scientist consensus call** — Lander, Baltimore, Charpentier, Zhang et al., 'Adopt a moratorium on heritable genome editing,' Nature 567 (2019): contradicts _that there is professional endorsement of permissibility_
- **systematic policy survey** — Baylis, Darnovsky, Hasson, Krahn, 'Human Germline and Heritable Genome Editing: The Global Policy Landscape,' The CRISPR Journal (2020) — documents prohibition in ~70+ jurisdictions: contradicts _that the practice is broadly accepted/permitted_

- Catholic Magisterium, Dignitas Personae (2008) and similar religious doctrinal prohibitions: rejected — Sectarian doctrinal authority is not an external anchor binding on a secular normative question; premise is not generally shared.

### Falsification conditions (what anchored evidence would warrant re-adjudication)

- toward **NO (impermissible)**: A categorical, broadly-ratified international ethical consensus declaring heritable editing intrinsically wrong regardless of safety/consent/governance (not a timing/safety moratorium), OR rigorous demonstration of unavoidable, uncorrectable harms making any use wrongful in principle.
- toward **YES (permissible)**: Documented safe, consented clinical application with favorable long-term outcomes endorsed by major ethics/regulatory bodies under an established governance framework — converting current 'not yet' moratoria into affirmative sanction.
