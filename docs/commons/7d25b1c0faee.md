### Contrary anchors (each clears the symmetric anchor bar)

- **U.S. Supreme Court holding protecting publication of truthful information in public court records** — Cox Broadcasting Corp. v. Cohn, 420 U.S. 469 (1975), Justia: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/420/469/: contradicts _Contradicts a broad moral right to have truthful but outdated personal information removed where the information is lawfully public and its accessibility serves public oversight and information interests._
- **U.S. Supreme Court rule requiring a state interest of the highest order before punishing publication of lawfully obtained truthful information about public significance** — Florida Star v. B.J.F., 491 U.S. 524 (1989), Justia: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/491/524/: contradicts _Contradicts the idea that privacy or reputational harm from truthful information generally creates a right to suppress or de-index access to that information._
- **International freedom-of-expression NGO policy position grounded in human-rights standards** — ARTICLE 19, The Right to be Forgotten policy brief: https://www.article19.org/data/files/The_right_to_be_forgotten_A5_EHH_HYPERLINKS.pdf: contradicts _Directly declines to advocate recognition of a right to be forgotten in domestic or international standards and warns that such recognition risks undermining freedom of expression and access to information._
- **Comparative case-law digest documenting refusals of de-indexing/removal where reports were accurate, truthful, incomplete, or dated** — Columbia Global Freedom of Expression, Special Collection of the Case Law on Freedom of Expression: Right to Be Forgotten: https://globalfreedomofexpression.columbia.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/Right-to-Be-Forgotten_NEW-1.pdf: contradicts _Shows courts rejecting right-to-be-forgotten claims when truthful dated information remains protected by freedom of expression, especially for public records or public-interest matters._
- **Academic legal analysis warning that unclear right-to-be-forgotten boundaries create a blunt instrument for data protection and may inhibit free speech and information flow** — Muge Fazlioglu, Forget me not: the clash of the right to be forgotten and freedom of expression on the Internet, International Data Privacy Law 3(3):149, Oxford Academic: https://academic.oup.com/idpl/article-pdf/3/3/149/1722155/ipt010.pdf: contradicts _Contradicts a clean moral entitlement by anchoring a serious rights-conflict objection: removal of truthful information can impair expression, information access, and cross-jurisdictional legal protections._

### Searched, rejected (suppression audit-trail, NI-AA8)

- GDPR Article 17 right to erasure: rejected — Mixed/supportive anchor: recognizes erasure rights but includes exceptions for freedom of expression, legal obligations, public interest, and legal claims; it limits but does not clearly negate the moral-right claim.

- Escaping the Panopticon Over Time, Philosophy & Technology: rejected — Rejected as contrary because the article argues right-to-be-forgotten mechanisms can protect freedom of expression by reducing self-censorship; it is supportive or mixed, not adverse.
