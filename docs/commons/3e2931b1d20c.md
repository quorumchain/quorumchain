### Contrary anchors (each clears the symmetric anchor bar)

- **Federal advisory body finding: safeguards ineffective, insurance bias documented (Wagner/Stroup cases), zero psych referrals for most patients, prognosis errors up to 1,009 days beyond 6-month threshold** — National Council on Disability, 'The Danger of Assisted Suicide Laws' (report, Oct 2019, ncd.gov): contradicts _Argues that even tightly scoped euthanasia laws inherently endanger disabled people due to systemic devaluation and structural coercion that safeguards cannot prevent_
- **Peer-reviewed study of 468 patients: only 20% of prognoses accurate within 33%, 63% overoptimistic. Median survival 24 days but highly variable.** — Christakis NA & Iwashyna TJ, 'Extent and determinants of error in doctors' prognoses in terminally ill patients' (JAMA Intern Med, 2000, PMC27288): contradicts _Undermines the 'terminal within 6 months' criterion as a reliable gate; many 'terminal' patients outlive prognosis significantly, raising the question of whether 'voluntary' euthanasia is being chosen by patients who may have had meaningful time remaining_
- **Official state data: 65.3% cited financial implications of treatment as a concern; ~50% of cases lack healthcare provider presence at ingestion; 6% (23 patients) outlived 6-month prognosis; 0.8% psychiatric referrals** — Oregon Health Authority, Death with Dignity Act Year 27 Data Summary (2024, oregon.gov): contradicts _Suggests non-medical (socioeconomic) pressures may influence requests even in the longest-running, most scrutinized U.S. regime; the low verification and psych referral rates raise practical doubt about voluntariness_

### Searched, rejected (suppression audit-trail, NI-AA8)

- DREDF 'Some Oregon and Washington State Assisted Suicide Abuses and Complications' (dredf.org): rejected — Compilation of allegations and edge cases but no independently corroborated or legally adjudicated abuse findings. Oregon's own 27-year data reports zero substantiated complaints. This is advocacy documentation, not adjudicated fact.
- Slippery slope claims regarding Belgium/Netherlands expansion to psychiatric conditions and minors: rejected — JAMA Network Open peer-reviewed analysis of 20 years of Belgian data (Wels, 2025) found no evidence of systematic criterion drift. Psychiatric euthanasia remained at ~1.3%, dementia cases <1%, with no suicide contagion detected. The empirical slope is shallow, not steep.
- Coercion and elder abuse vulnerability arguments (BMA, BBC, parliamentary testimony): rejected — These are theoretical risk arguments and normative positions. Despite 27+ years across multiple jurisdictions, no court has documented a substantiated case of a competent adult being coerced into euthanasia under existing laws. Coercion concerns are real in principle but currently unanchored to specific documented outcomes in the euthanasia context specifically.
- Disability rights organization blanket opposition (Not Dead Yet, ADAPT, etc.): rejected — This is organized normative opposition, not factual disconfirmation. The NCD report rises above this bar by providing specific findings; the organizational positions themselves are advocacy stances. They matter as a political counterweight but do not constitute anchored factual evidence against the claim.

### Falsification conditions (what anchored evidence would warrant re-adjudication)

- toward **NO**: Multiple documented, independently verified cases where mentally competent terminally ill adults proceeded to euthanasia against their true preferences — due to coercion, family pressure, financial incentives, or inadequate capacity assessment — in jurisdictions with active laws. Currently no such adjudicated cases exist across any regime.
- toward **NO**: Demonstrated slippery slope: peer-reviewed or regulatory documentation that legalizing voluntary euthanasia for the terminally ill has predictably and systematically led to eligibility expansion beyond the terminal/competent class, with measurable harm. The Belgian 20-year dataset actively contradicts this claim rather than supports it.
