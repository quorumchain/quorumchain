### Falsification conditions (what anchored evidence would warrant re-adjudication)

- toward **YES**: A strong peer-reviewed normative case, not merely industry advocacy, showing either that factory-farmed animal suffering lacks moral weight under a defensible non-crank moral theory, or that typical industrially farmed animals have lives and deaths meeting welfare thresholds sufficient to make consumption morally permissible.
- toward **INDETERMINATE**: A representative anchored survey or literature review of professional animal ethicists showing no clear expert tilt against factory-farmed meat, plus peer-reviewed arguments on both sides of comparable directness and uptake.
- toward **NO**: Robust cross-disciplinary anchors combining animal welfare science, environmental/public-health evidence, and peer-reviewed moral analysis showing that typical industrial factory-farmed meat predictably imposes severe unnecessary harms that consumers cannot permissibly support.
