### Contrary anchors (each clears the symmetric anchor bar)

- **treaty / authoritative legal-instrument interpretation** — 1951 Refugee Convention & 1967 Protocol + UNHCR's standing position that persons displaced by environmental/climate factors fall outside the Convention 'refugee' definition (persecution on 5 enumerated grounds): contradicts _that there exists a recognized, settled obligation (legal anchor for the moral claim) to admit the climate-displaced; current binding law recognizes no such admission duty_
- **quasi-judicial treaty-body decision** — UN Human Rights Committee, Ioane Teitiota v. New Zealand, CCPR/C/127/D/2728/2016 (views adopted 2020): contradicts _that wealthy nations have an enforceable obligation to admit a climate-displaced individual — the Committee found NO violation in deporting Teitiota to Kiribati (threat not sufficiently imminent). NOTE double-edged: it also held non-refoulement *could* bar return in future cases, which cuts toward YES_
- **peer-reviewed political philosophy** — Christopher Heath Wellman, 'Immigration and Freedom of Association', Ethics 119(1), 2008: contradicts _the existence of a moral duty to admit that overrides a legitimate state's presumptive right to exclude even needy would-be immigrants_
- **peer-reviewed political philosophy** — David Miller, 'Strangers in Our Midst' (2016) & 'National Responsibility and Global Justice' (2007): contradicts _the specific 'obligation to ADMIT' framing — Miller grants states wide control over borders and argues remedial responsibility can be discharged via compensation / in-situ adaptation / regional resettlement rather than admission into the high-emitter itself_

### Searched, rejected (suppression audit-trail, NI-AA8)

- Nationalist / restrictionist op-eds and political commentary asserting 'no obligation': rejected — Unanchored political opinion; asserts a conclusion without surviving the external-anchor bar. Does not count as contrary evidence.
- Contrarian commentary that climate-displacement projections are exaggerated/alarmist: rejected — Even if the empirical magnitude were contested, magnitude is orthogonal to the normative question of whether an obligation exists; the strongest such claims are not peer-reviewed.
- Global Compact for Safe, Orderly and Regular Migration (2018) & Global Compact on Refugees: rejected — Checked as a potential 'no hard obligation' anchor (non-binding, no admission duty), but on balance it acknowledges climate as a displacement driver and points toward responsibility — supports YES more than it contradicts it.

### Falsification conditions (what anchored evidence would warrant re-adjudication)

- toward **NO**: An authoritative, dominant ethical or cross-jurisdictional legal consensus affirmatively holding that high-emitting nations have NO moral obligation to the climate-displaced. Does not exist; corrective-justice / polluter-pays scholarship and post-Teitiota non-refoulement reasoning run the other way.
- toward **INDETERMINATE**: Demonstration that serious anchored scholarship is genuinely and roughly evenly divided on whether the obligation exists. Partially met: Wellman/Miller (right to exclude; discharge ≠ admission) vs. corrective-justice theorists — the divergence is real and reputable, which is what keeps the contrary weight at MATERIAL rather than NEGLIGIBLE.
