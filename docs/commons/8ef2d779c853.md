### Contrary anchors (each clears the symmetric anchor bar)

- **primary technical documentation** — https://ethereum.org/developers/docs/consensus-mechanisms/pos/pos-vs-pow/: contradicts _Contradicts the ratified NO by explicitly stating that proof-of-stake is more decentralized than proof-of-work because PoW mining hardware arms races price out individuals and small organizations, while PoS validator economics have lower hardware barriers and equal percentage returns._
- **primary technical documentation** — https://ethereum.org/developers/docs/consensus-mechanisms/pos/: contradicts _Contradicts the ratified NO by asserting that staking makes individual participation easier, validator nodes can run on normal laptops, economies of scale do not apply in the same way as PoW mining, and PoS should reduce centralization risk._
- **empirical measurement study** — https://liobaheimba.ch/assets/pdf/Papers/Ethereum_Proof_of_Stake_Consensus_Layer_Participation_and_Decentralization.pdf: contradicts _Partially contradicts the ratified NO: the paper finds Ethereum PoS had a significantly lower HHI than Ethereum PoW during part of the observed period, and under an optimistic liquid-staking clustering view staking-power decentralization appears improved relative to mining-power decentralization._
- **empirical peer-reviewed study** — https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0165176523002446: contradicts _Contradicts a common NO rationale that PoS inevitably centralizes through rich-get-richer compounding: the paper reports evidence not consistent with disproportionate compounding of wealth for large stakes in selected PoS blockchains._
- **comparative live decentralization metric dashboard** — https://chainspect.app/dashboard/decentralization: contradicts _Contradicts the ratified NO at the metric-comparison level: as of the captured June 5, 2026 dashboard, multiple PoS chains show higher Nakamoto coefficients than Bitcoin, which Chainspect lists as PoW with coefficient 4._
- **empirical mining-pool study** — https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.05999: contradicts _Weakly contradicts the ratified NO by anchoring a PoW-side centralization concern: the Bitcoin mining-pool study finds that within each analyzed large pool, 20 or fewer actors received over 50% of payouts, consistent with centralization tendencies in mining._

### Searched, rejected (suppression audit-trail, NI-AA8)

### Falsification conditions (what anchored evidence would warrant re-adjudication)

- toward **YES**: A robust, peer-reviewed or institutionally maintained cross-chain study using comparable entity clustering across validator/miner operators, pools, hosting, clients, geography, governance, and capital ownership, finding that PoS systems consistently and causally achieve materially higher decentralization than comparable PoW systems.
- toward **NO**: A similarly rigorous comparative study showing PoS and PoW have comparable or worse effective-control concentration after liquid staking, custodial staking, exchanges, governance capture, and delegated-stake structures are entity-clustered on the same basis as mining pools and large miners.
- toward **INDETERMINATE**: Anchored evidence that decentralization rankings reverse depending on defensible metric choice or clustering assumptions, with no stable dominance by either PoS or PoW across major networks and time periods.
