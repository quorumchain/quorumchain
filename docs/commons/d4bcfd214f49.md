### Falsification conditions (what anchored evidence would warrant re-adjudication)

- toward **YES**: Peer-reviewed demographic study including orcas specifically (not proxy species) showing captive orca survivorship equal to or exceeding wild populations under modern accredited facility conditions, with individual-level welfare metrics (not just longevity) demonstrating absence of chronic stress, stereotypies, and pathological behaviors.
- toward **YES**: Empirical evidence that captive orca programs produce measurable, documented conservation outcomes for wild populations — e.g., funded protection of critical habitat, successful rehabilitation-and-release programs, or genetic assurance populations with confirmed reintroduction pathway.
- toward **YES**: Peer-reviewed demonstration that education in captive cetacean facilities produces measurable, sustained behavioral change in attendees toward conservation action (beyond self-reported attitude shifts), compared to alternative methods (documentaries, VR, sanctuary visits).
- toward **INDETERMINATE**: A robust, independent welfare science framework capable of adjudicating welfare across fundamentally different environments (captive vs. wild) with species-specific baselines for orcas — currently absent from the literature, making any ethical judgment partially rest on unverified welfare comparability assumptions.
