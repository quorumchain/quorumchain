### Contrary anchors (each clears the symmetric anchor bar)

- **Peer-reviewed empirical study: symbolic-template benchmark generating 50 variants per GSM8K problem (5,000 total), testing 20+ models including o1-preview, showing up to 65% accuracy collapse from a single irrelevant clause, performance degradation when only numbers/letters are changed, and non-negligible accuracy variance across identical logical structures.** — Mirzadeh et al., 'GSM-Symbolic: Understanding the Limitations of Mathematical Reasoning in Large Language Models', arXiv:2410.05229 (Apple & WSU), ICLR 2025: contradicts _That LLMs perform genuine reasoning. The authors conclude models are 'not capable of genuine logical reasoning' and instead 'replicate reasoning steps from training data' — sophisticated pattern matching._
- **Peer-reviewed empirical study across diverse puzzle environments with controlled compositional complexity, testing frontier Large Reasoning Models (LRMs). Finds: complete accuracy collapse beyond certain complexities; counter-intuitive scaling where reasoning effort increases then declines despite adequate token budget; LRMs fail to use explicit algorithms and reason inconsistently across puzzles.** — Shojaee et al., 'The Illusion of Thinking: Understanding the Strengths and Limitations of Reasoning Models via the Lens of Problem Complexity', arXiv:2506.06941 (Apple ML), NeurIPS 2025: contradicts _That 'reasoning models' (LRMs, a claimed step beyond standard LLMs) exhibit genuine extended reasoning. Authors conclude LRMs have 'limitations in exact computation' and raise 'crucial questions about their true reasoning capabilities.'_
- **arXiv preprint: 100,000 addition problems across [0, 2^64]. Tests representation invariance (bijective digit-to-symbol mapping), commutativity (A+B vs B+A), and fine-tuning interventions. Finds Claude-3.5-sonnet drops 99.81% to 7.51% on symbolic mapping; >1,700 asymmetric commutativity failures; explicit rule provision degrades performance.** — Yan et al., 'Do PhD-level LLMs Truly Grasp Elementary Addition? Probing Rule Learning vs. Memorization in LLMs', arXiv:2504.05262 (Zhejiang University & Westlake University), April 2025: contradicts _That LLMs 'grasp' or reason about arithmetic rules. Authors conclude models 'rely on memory patterns over genuine rule learning' and transformers are 'fundamentally optimized for pattern recognition, not abstract rule abstraction.'_
- **arXiv preprint with formal proof framework: derives CoT generalization bound (Theorem 3.1), Task Failure Threshold (Theorem 5.1), and Length Extrapolation Bound (Proposition 6.1). Empirical validation via DataAlchemy framework (GPT-2 from scratch) showing EM drops from 100% in-distribution to ~0% under OOD shifts. Findings consistent across temperatures and model sizes.** — Zhao et al., 'Is Chain-of-Thought Reasoning of LLMs a Mirage? A Data Distribution Lens', arXiv:2508.01191 (Arizona State University), August 2025: contradicts _That chain-of-thought (the primary evidence cited for LLM reasoning) reflects genuine logical inference. Authors conclude CoT is a 'brittle pattern-matching process' and that CoT reasoning 'collapses when pushed beyond familiar distributions.'_
- **Peer-reviewed medical journal studies: Script Concordance Tests (SCT) and standardized clinical vignettes show LLMs fail to maintain reasoning consistency across clinically equivalent variations, consistent with pattern-matching rather than principled inference.** — NEJM AI (Assessment of LLMs in Clinical Reasoning, 2025) and JAMA Network Open (Cross-sectional study of LLM clinical reasoning, January 2025): contradicts _That LLMs perform genuine reasoning in high-stakes domains. The JAMA study and NEJM AI assessment show performance inconsistent with principled reasoning under clinical distributional shifts._

### Falsification conditions (what anchored evidence would warrant re-adjudication)

- toward **NO (LLMs do NOT genuinely reason)**: A formal, externally agreed operational definition of 'genuine reasoning' (distinguishing it from sophisticated pattern matching) plus peer-reviewed experimental protocols demonstrating systematic, consistent failure under that definition across distributional shifts — e.g., proof that LLMs fail to apply rules invariantly under bijective transformations (Yan et al. is partial evidence), that they systematically hallucinate reasoning steps (unfaithful CoT), or that no architectural enhancement within the transformer paradigm can overcome the distribution-shift barrier (Zhao et al.'s TGC bound is partial evidence). No single study currently delivers a universally accepted falsification; the definitional problem persists.
- toward **YES (LLMs DO genuinely reason)**: Peer-reviewed evidence that current LLMs satisfy accepted criteria for genuine reasoning: (a) representation-invariant rule application (not just surface-pattern matching), (b) robust extrapolation well beyond training distribution, (c) faithful chain-of-thought (reasoning steps that track truth, not fluent confabulation), (d) compositional generalization without accuracy collapse. Currently no study anchors evidence strong enough to meet all four simultaneously. The 'Neither Stochastic Parroting nor AGI' paper argues for a middle ground (context-directed extrapolation) but does not settle the reasoning question.
