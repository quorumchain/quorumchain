## Adversarial review (CIP-10 auditor)

### Contrary anchors (each clears the symmetric anchor bar)

- **government-run randomized controlled trial** — Kela (Finnish Social Insurance Institution), Basic Income Experiment 2017–2018 final report (2020); RCT, ~2000 unemployed: contradicts _Premise that unconditional cash reduces work effort and harms wellbeing — found neutral-to-slightly-positive employment plus significant wellbeing/mental-health gains_
- **quasi-experimental econometric study of a real unconditional, universal, permanent dividend** — Jones & Marinescu, 'The Labor Market Impacts of Universal and Permanent Cash Transfers' (AEJ: Economic Policy 2022 / NBER 2018), Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend: contradicts _Premise that an unconditional universal cash transfer reduces aggregate employment — found no significant aggregate labor-supply reduction_
- **large multi-year randomized controlled trial** — Banerjee, Niehaus, Suri et al., GiveDirectly long-term UBI RCT, Kenya (NBER working papers, ongoing): contradicts _Premise that recipients stop working or squander unconditional transfers — found productive investment, no decline in labor supply_
- **controlled field study** — West, Baker et al., Stockton Economic Empowerment Demonstration (SEED) preliminary analysis (2021), $500/month guaranteed income: contradicts _Premise of work disincentive — full-time employment rose faster among recipients than controls_

### Searched, rejected (suppression audit-trail, NI-AA8)

- Seattle/Denver Income Maintenance Experiments (SIME/DIME) and 1968–80 US/Canada Negative Income Tax trials: rejected — Cuts toward NO, not contrary — showed measurable secondary-earner work reduction; the marital-dissolution finding was later disputed/reanalyzed, so it neither cleanly supports nor undermines the verdict
- Advocacy/think-tank UBI affordability costings (e.g., revenue-neutral funding models): rejected — Projection models, not realized outcomes; do not anchor real-world fiscal sustainability of a universal-permanent program at scale
- All UBI pilots collectively (Finland, Stockton, GiveDirectly, Ontario, etc.) as evidence for the full claim: rejected — Scope mismatch — targeted and/or time-limited samples; none is genuinely universal, unconditional, and permanent at national scale, so none anchors macro-level inflation, full-population labor supply, or long-run fiscal effects
- Normative authorities (e.g., Van Parijs/Standing pro-UBI philosophy): rejected — Normative argument, not an external factual anchor; per frozen criteria no anchor settles the moral 'should'
