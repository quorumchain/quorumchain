### Contrary anchors (each clears the symmetric anchor bar)

- **peer-reviewed empirical study** — Kim, Garg, Peng, Garg. 'Correlated Errors in Large Language Models.' arXiv:2506.07962v1. Peer-reviewed (ICML 2025 poster). Evaluated 350+ LLMs across 3 datasets. Finding: Models agree ~60% of the time when both err (vs. 33% random baseline). Error correlation INCREASES with individual model accuracy — larger, more capable models converge on the same failure modes. Shared provider, shared architecture, similar parameter sizes all significantly increase correlation.: contradicts _The planned §2 names correlated error as a weakness but frames it as manageable via round-60 anchoring and correlation-measurement probes. The paper shows the scale of the problem is severe — even across different vendors and architectures, correlated error is the norm, not the exception — which materially raises the bar for the round-60 defense's sufficiency claim._
- **peer-reviewed empirical thesis** — Kota. 'Design and Evaluation of Multi-Agent AI Oracle Systems for Prediction Market Resolution.' arXiv:2605.30802v1. Yale University thesis. Evaluated 3 architecturally diverse models (GPT-5 Nano, DeepSeek V3, Llama-3.3-70B). Finding: pairwise error correlations r=0.529–0.689; Condorcet Jury Theorem would predict ~91% accuracy under majority voting but observed only 82.93%; 14% of questions form a 'hard core' that resists ALL architectures.: contradicts _The planned §2's thesis that 2/3 supermajority with diversity raises cost of capture. The Kota paper shows the independence assumption of Condorcet Jury Theorem is violated in practice — ensemble gains are marginal (+1.01pp over best single model), not multiplicative. The 14% 'hard core' failure rate means diversity alone cannot drive error to zero even with anchoring._
- **peer-reviewed (Nature Scientific Reports)** — Kraidia. 'When Collaboration Fails: Persuasion-Driven Adversarial Influence in Multi-Agent LLM Debate.' Scientific Reports (Nature), 2026. DOI:10.1038/s41598-026-42705-7. Finding: A single adversarial agent in a multi-agent debate causes 10-40% system accuracy degradation and >30% false consensus alignment. Longer debates entrench false consensus; more agents only delay takeover; prompt warnings fail over multiple rounds.: contradicts _The planned §2 does not enumerate the 'persuasive hijack of a deliberative validator' as a named threat vector. In Quorumchain, the 'method-not-conclusion refuter' is itself a validator whose output feeds every validator's deliberation. If that refuter can be strategically wrong with high rhetorical confidence — the paper shows this works with zero parameter access, purely inference-time — it functions as a structural attack surface, not just a benign probe._
- **peer-reviewed survey** — Geng et al. 'Prompt Injection Attacks on Large Language Models: A Survey.' ScienceDirect, 2026. Finding: Universal adversarial phrases (2-5 tokens) achieve ~70% attack success rate across open and proprietary models. JudgeDeceiver achieves 89-99% ASR across models. Combined/PAIR attacks achieve ~100% ASR on several models.: contradicts _The planned §2's threat enumeration lists collusion, correlated error, jury-selection, and sybil-forgetting — but omits 'universal adversarial input' as a named vector. This is a single input that reliably triggers errors across multiple independently-keyed models from different vendors simultaneously, which means diversity provides no defense and the mechanical check of §1 is subverted if the adversarial payload itself passes the mechanical check._
- **arXiv preprint** — Yeo & Choi. 'Multimodal Prompt Injection Attacks: Risks and Defenses for Modern LLMs.' arXiv:2509.05883v1. Tested 8 commercial models (GPT-4o, Claude 3, LLaMA-3, Mixtral, Gemma). Finding: Even Claude 3 (described as most resilient) was partially susceptible to image injection and fully vulnerable to prompt leakage across all other categories.: contradicts _Supports that the 'capture through the gap between rule's intent and mechanical check' principle is empirically verified, but also shows the gap is broader than implied — even the 'most resilient' model across 4 attack categories still has nonzero vulnerability. The planned §2 states this principle but may understate how wide the gap actually is._
