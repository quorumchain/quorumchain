## 1. Problem

[[CIP-9]] §3/§6 promised a claim could be **superseded with a reason** and carry its
**history**, but deferred the mechanism at v0.1. Without it, a later verdict on the same
claim either silently overwrites the prior (anti-Orwell violation) or sits unrelated to it.
Two failure modes had to be closed at once: (a) an `EMPIRICAL_LIVE` claim presented as
atemporally true, and (b) a re-adjudication that **buries** a claim by silently re-typing it.

## 2. Red-team → fold (the workflow that produced the ratified text)

The first draft was red-teamed on ballot `c5439212ea46…` and returned **FAILS 2/3**
(V1 FAILS, V2 FAILS, V3 HOLDS). Two material flaws were named and folded:

- **V1's flaw** — NI-13e advertised "no burying a claim by re-typing it" but did not
  *enforce* it; a supersede could clear the anchor bar on substance while silently carrying a
  different `epistemicType`. **Folded as NI-13h** (type-invariant lineage; mismatch rejected
  at admission even with a valid anchor).
- **V2's flaw** — the anchor gate was deferred to v0.2 while v0.1 shipped the lineage +
  "current = latest by log order", leaving a window where any later ballot becomes current
  before the gate exists. **Folded** by making the anchor gate a **v0.1 admission/projection
  rule** and making the competing-successor head rule explicit (NI-13g).

## 3. Spec / invariants introduced — NI-13a … NI-13h

- **NI-13a** — **type is frozen and signed, never inferred.** `epistemicType` is part of the
  hashed ballot; the projection reads it and never guesses. *(The "type is SIGNED" ideal that
  [[CIP-14]] later fully closes by binding the type into the ballot hash.)*
- **NI-13b** — **type modulates presentation, not computation.** Type / time / lineage never
  change any ballot's `status` / `verdict` / `stances` / `standing`.
- **NI-13c** — **no empirical claim is ever atemporally RESOLVED.** An `EMPIRICAL_LIVE`
  resolution always carries its `evidenceTime` and is presented as point-in-time.
- **NI-13d** — **supersession appends, never edits.** A superseding verdict is a NEW signed
  ballot referencing the prior; the prior verdict and its evidence remain intact and readable.
- **NI-13e** — **re-adjudication is anchor-gated, from v0.1.** Promoting a supersede to
  `lineage.current` for a SETTLED / EMPIRICAL_LIVE claim requires a qualifying anchor. *(Later
  hardened by [[CIP-15]] from an unverified `newAnchor:boolean` to a typed, structurally
  verified anchor object.)*
- **NI-13f** — **normative consensus is conventional, never truth.** A NORMATIVE
  supermajority is recorded as "the panel's majority position as of D", not as fact.
- **NI-13g** — **lineage is determined only by signed pointers + log order.** "Current" vs
  "superseded" is computed deterministically from the chain — no editor, no off-chain
  ordering; the latest valid successor in log order is current. Divergence the head rule does
  not subsume is a [[CIP-5]] fork (two readable lineages), never a silent pick.
- **NI-13h** — **epistemic type is invariant across a supersession lineage.** *(Folded from
  the c5439212 red-team, V1.)* A `supersedes` ballot MUST carry the **same** `epistemicType`
  as the ballot it supersedes; a mismatch is rejected at admission **even with a valid
  anchor** — a type change must be a [[CIP-5]] fork or a panel-ratified sub-claim, never a
  ride-along on a substance supersede.

## 4. Compatibility / migration

Append-only / forward-only preserved: re-adjudication is implemented as a new signed ballot
referencing the prior, never a mutation. The change is a [[CIP-9]] read-path extension and
touches no T0 frozen-unamendable core item.
