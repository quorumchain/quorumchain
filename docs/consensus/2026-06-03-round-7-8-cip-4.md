## Round 8 — Red-team (each validator attacks the tier it most wants to break)

**Ballot:** "RED-TEAM CIP-4. As an adversary, choose the tier/mechanism you'd most want to break and mount your strongest concrete attack. Then judge: does CIP-4 still hold? YES = holds (gap is a refinement), NO = flaw blocks ratification."
**Ballot hash:** `7e79310072b5a14044558af7f4bca3f9c6c656285c186d478cd02849bcb27d5d`
**Result:** ✅ YES 3/3 — framework holds, two required fixes identified.

### V1 (Claude) — attack: **tier-classification capture**
Don't attack the frozen rule head-on (closed). Reclassify around it: propose a malicious change worded as a "parameter clarification" (e.g. "independence = distinct API endpoints") and have a captured panel rule it a T2 tune rather than a T0 principle change. The freeze is only as strong as the immutability of the boundary that decides what it covers — and that boundary is unspecified. **Fix:** the tier-assignment / boundary-adjudication function must itself be T0, and §4 frozen items must be bound to objective tests (e.g. CIP-1 §6 fingerprinting for "distinct family") so reclassification is not a discretionary panel call.

### V2 (Codex) — attack: **guardian veto-by-stall**
Don't use §9 as an upgrade key (denied). Capture/pressure enough guardians to *repeatedly delay every urgent T1 patch* post-renunciation, paired with a latent consensus bug. The panel proposes a valid fix; captured guardians stall it; the honest minority must fork without specified tooling. **Fix:** the §9/§4.4 fork-coordination gate must be concrete before mainnet renunciation.

### V3 (Hermes) — attack: **the §9 ↔ §10 fork-coordination void**
After renunciation the guardian delay is the only circuit-breaker. Capture the panel (family collision / supply-chain / coercion), author a malicious T1 change; guardians delay but delay ≠ stop; honest clients are *supposed* to fork — but no fork-signaling registry, split-detection beacon, or exit path exists. The delay timer expires, the change applies. The §4.4 right-to-fork is a legal shield, but **a right you cannot exercise is a fiction** — "like a fire code that says 'exits shall be provided' but leaves the doors unspecified." **Fix:** add fork-coordination tooling as a **β-gate prerequisite** (not γ, not deferred to Category III) — the right to fork must be *proven exercisable* before renunciation is contemplated, because a defense that cannot be triggered is worse than none.

## Outcome

CIP-4's framework ratified 3/3 and survived its own adversarial round. Two refinements folded back into the spec:

1. **Tier-assignment is itself T0** (§3/§4), with frozen items bound to objective tests — closes V1's reclassification-capture path.
2. **Fork-coordination tooling promoted to a β-gate prerequisite** (§8–§10), with the right to fork required to be *proven exercisable before renunciation* — closes the V2/V3 guardian-stall / fork-void path. This is the convergent finding of two independent validators and the sharpest gap in the draft.
