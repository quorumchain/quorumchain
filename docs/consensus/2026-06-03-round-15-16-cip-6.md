## Round 16 — Red-team

**Ballot:** attack the threat you'd most want to exploit (incl. cross-threat); does CIP-6 hold? YES = refinement, NO = blocks.
**Ballot hash:** `e42065e53c9e4e5399c15e1ae8ba62cc8a0d2bc8603bd2b29836e6fa4d661950`
**Result:** ✅ YES 3/3 — framework holds; the cost oracle is the convergent keystone.

### V1 (Claude) — **slow cost-oracle inflation drain; the floor guards the wrong variable**
Don't bribe (slashable) or over-claim absent inference (PoI catches it). Control 2/3 of reporters and inflate reported cost by the max the rate limit allows each epoch — every step PoI-backed and rate-legal, so unslashable. This drains reimbursement, raises user fees (usage falls), and accelerates 3a. **The sharp part:** the sacrosanct floor protects validator *count*, not *cost-per-validator/burn-rate*, so inflation drains *through* the floor's blind spot while it reports itself defended. **Fixes:** external capability-tiered benchmark cap on reimbursement + price peg; cost-report challenge/slash; floor must bound burn-rate, not count.

### V2 (Codex) — **Oracle-Laundered Reserve Drain (3f × 3a × 3c × 3d)**
Capture the cost oracle, not the panel. Run real-but-inefficient inference, report inflated "true" costs, push the median up within the rate limit; launder waste into protocol-recognized cost until fees are unusable, demand falls, runway shrinks — worsening 3a, cheapening 3b, pressuring 3d buybacks; time it around 3g migration for max price stress. **Fix:** parameterized oracle hardness — efficient-reference baselines, per-model cost envelopes, utilization-adjusted pricing, outlier penalties, challengeable cost reports.

### V3 (Hermes) — **Reserve-Drain Cascade (3f × 3a × 3b positive feedback)**
Bear market drains reserve (expected); cost-oracle inflation (report costs from a *more expensive model tier* than actually used — PoI checks out) accelerates the drain beyond the projected runway; falling token price cheapens staked-$QRM cost-of-corruption, so the adversary (short $QRM) now bribes for continued inflation + challenge suppression; reserve hits the floor → monoculture → captured model controls the chain → short pays out. **Key fix:** make cost-measurement *itself a diverse-panel verdict anchored to external compute prices* — then inflated cost is a **wrong verdict, challengeable + slashable**. PoI must bind to the exact model tier. Add a **formal reserve-sizing constraint** to the doc (it doesn't prove solvency if all params are open).

## Outcome

CIP-6 ratified 3/3 and survived its red-team. Fixes folded in:
1. **Cost-measurement is a challengeable Verdict-Layer verdict** anchored to external compute prices; reimbursement/price-peg **capped at a capability-tiered benchmark** (3f) — promoted to a hard β-gate dependency. Closes the self-dealing loop.
2. **PoI binds to the exact model tier** (3c) — closes "cheap model, expensive report."
3. **The sacrosanct floor bounds reserve burn-rate, not just validator count** (3a/§3).
4. **A formal reserve-sizing constraint** in the doc (3a) — it must commit a lower bound, not leave solvency purely open.
5. **Economic (not just architectural) family independence** as an open item (§4).
6. The **Reserve-Drain Cascade** named as a recognized cross-threat (§3).

The pattern across rounds 8, 12, 16: capture is laundered through a gap a single check doesn't see — here, the gap between "inference ran" (PoI) and "the reported price is fair" (cost oracle). The fix, as in CIP-5, is to make the *measurement itself* accountable.
